---

## Primary ICP (priority #1)

**Men who want a leaner, cleaner silhouette under everyday and dress shirts — without padded “fake muscle” undershirts.**

Shared traits: they notice how they look in a fitted tee or button-down; they value discretion; they’ll pay for premium feel if the product is honest and works.

### Profile

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Age** | ~25–45 (core), open 22–55 |
| **Gender** | Men (CoreFit line) |
| **Income** | Mid disposable income; quality-per-dollar mindset |
| **Behavior** | Mobile-first; discovers via Instagram/TikTok/search; reads reviews and sizing notes before buy |
| **Motivation** | Look put-together and confident in clothes they already own |
| **Decision style** | Compares tiers; often starts Essential or Pro; upgrades after proof |

### Jobs to be done

- Flatten chest contour under fitted shirts
- Smooth midsection without bulk or visible lines
- Wear something invisible under polos, tees, and dress shirts
- Feel confident at work, dates, events, travel — not “in costume”

---

## Their problems (pain order)

1. **Visible chest / soft midsection under fitted clothes**
   Fitted tees and dress shirts telegraph contour they don’t want. Baggy clothes feel like giving up.

2. **Distrust of gimmick products**
   Amazon “abs shirts,” foam pads, and fake-muscle tanks feel embarrassing and dishonest. They’ve been burned or laughed at the category.

3. **Sizing and hold uncertainty**
   Don’t know if compression will be too weak, too tight, or show lines. Need clear fit guidance and a low-risk entry.

4. **All-day comfort vs. hold tradeoff**
   Office and travel days need odor control, moisture management, and seams that stay invisible for 8–12+ hours.

5. **Discreet shopping / packaging**
   Don’t want loud labels or packaging that outs the category.

---

## What we deliver (outcomes)

- **Flatter silhouette** via targeted chest + core compression zones
- **Invisible under clothes** — tank cut, seamless sides (tier-dependent), zero bulk
- **Honest ladder** — Essential ($49) → Pro ($89) → Elite ($149) → Signature ($229)
- **Premium materials** that feel intentional (Italian microfiber, Japanese cooling yarn, Swiss-weave)
- **Risk reducers** — clear sizing, 30-day trial language, discreet packaging

**Core promise:** Look leaner and more confident in the shirts you already wear — with real compression, not pads.

---

## Central argument: honesty over gimmicks

This category is polluted by fake muscle. Our wedge is product truth:

> Not padded. Not fake muscle. Real compression.

Lead with that whenever skepticism shows up (ads comments, FAQs, PDP).

Broader Milan Hype fashion personas (Style Setter, Occasion Shopper) apply when expanding beyond CoreFit — see `docs/milan-hype-brand-strategy-and-blueprint.md`.

## What we never do to this audience

- Shame body type or use fear-based fat-shaming
- Promise plastic surgery results or “instant abs”
- Hide that higher tiers hold firmer (tier honesty)
- Push aggressive discounting that makes the brand feel cheap
