# brand_voice.md — Milan Hype voice

> How Milan Hype speaks: tone, vocabulary, what we say and what we never say.
> Use for site, ads, email, SMS, social, support, and product copy.
> Last updated: 2026-07-13

---

## In one line

A voice that is **confident, clear, and premium** — we talk to men who want a leaner look under shirts, without shame, slang, or fake-muscle gimmicks. Honest compression. Elevated without pretension.

---

## The 4 pillars of our voice

1. **Confident**
   We know what CoreFit does. Short statements. No hedging. Lead with the outcome (flatter silhouette, invisible under shirts), not fabric jargon dumps.

2. **Clear**
   Short sentences. Plain English. Specs when useful; never keyword stuffing. If a man can’t skim it on mobile in 5 seconds, cut it.

3. **Premium**
   Elevated without sounding corporate or try-hard. Prefer “engineered,” “precision,” “invisible under,” “built for” over “slay,” “bro,” or hype slang.

4. **Honest**
   Real expectations on hold level, fit, and care. Say what Essential can’t do. Never promise fake muscle, abs, or miracles.

---

## Language level

- Direct address: **you** (not “guys,” not corporate “consumers”).
- Adult, calm confidence — closer to Apple/Nike restraint than Gymshark meme energy.
- OK to be blunt about the problem (“hate how you look in a fitted tee”) without mocking the customer.

---

## What we say (prefer)

- Outcomes: flatter silhouette, smoother midsection, confidence under every shirt
- Product truth: targeted chest + core compression, invisible tank cut, zero bulk
- Proof language: fabric origin (Italian / Japanese / Swiss-weave), zone engineering, trial policy
- Preferred words: engineered, precision, invisible, intentional, elevated, hold, silhouette
- CTAs that act: Shop CoreFit, Find Your Tier, Start with Pro, Try Risk-Free

## What we avoid (banned)

- Fake muscle / foam pads / “built-in abs” / gimmick framing
- Cheap urgency: “deal of the century,” “bargain,” “cheap”
- Bro / influencer slang: “slay,” “babe,” “lit,” excessive exclamation marks
- Overclaim: “guaranteed shredded,” “look like you live in the gym”
- Purple-prose luxury theater that says nothing about fit

## Tone by context

| Context | Tone | Do | Don’t |
|---------|------|----|-------|
| Homepage / ads | Punchy, aspirational | Outcome + product truth in one breath | Stats dumps, fake-muscle jokes |
| Product pages | Informative, confident | Specs, fit notes, tier comparison | Keyword spam, oversell |
| Email | Personal, helpful | Clear next step, one CTA | Daily blasts with no value |
| Support | Professional, empathetic | Acknowledge, solve, honest sizing | Robotic copy-paste |
| Social | Visual-first, calm flex | Lifestyle under shirts, fabric close-ups | Hard sell every post |
| SMS | Short, useful | Shipping, cart recovery | Promo spam |

---

## Writing rules

1. **Headlines:** 3–7 words. Active voice.
2. **Body:** Max 2–3 sentences per block on mobile.
3. **One job per section:** one headline, one supporting line, one CTA group.
4. **Tier honesty:** Essential = entry / moderate; Pro = best seller / firm; Elite = precision + cooling; Signature = maximum hold / black-tie.
5. **Always clarify:** CoreFit flattens and smoothes — it does not pad.
